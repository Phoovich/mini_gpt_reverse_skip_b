Batch Padding

Sequences in a batch must have the same length. Shorter sequences are padded with <PAD> tokens; the model ignores these via a src_key_padding_mask.

Loss ignores PAD: Cross-entropy is computed with ignore_index=PAD_ID, so padding positions don't affect learning.

Step 3 · Phase 1

Step 4 · Phase 2

GRPO — Group Relative Policy Optimization

For each training prompt, roll out the policy 4 times, compute rewards, normalize advantages within the group, then update the policy to favor higher-rewarded outputs.

4 Rollouts from 1 Prompt
Hover over a rollout node for details.
Advantage Normalization
Ai = (ri − μ) / (σ + 1e-8)
Loss Breakdown
policy_loss=−Σ(log_prob × A) / 32
kl_loss=0.2 × Σ(KL) / 8
entropy_bonus=−0.005 × Σ(H) / 32
loss=policy_loss + kl_loss + entropy_bonus
Why Group Relative?

Advantages are normalized within the group of 4, not across the entire batch. This stabilizes training: a rollout is "good" or "bad" relative to its siblings from the same prompt, not an absolute scale.

KL Divergence Penalty

The KL penalty keeps the RL policy from drifting too far from the reference SFT model, preventing catastrophic forgetting of the reversal task while learning to skip 'b'.

Policy vs Reference Distribution
Policy drift 10%
Reference model (SFT) Current policy KL contribution
KL Divergence Value

Loss contribution
kl_loss = 0.2 × 0.00 = 0.00
kl_coef 0.20
Formula
KL(π∥πref) = Σ π(a|s) log[π(a|s)/πref(a|s)]
Why It Matters

Without KL penalty, the RL policy would optimize reward at any cost — possibly forgetting how to reverse sequences at all. The penalty anchors it to the SFT starting point.

Implementation
kl = (probs * (log_probs − ref_log_probs)).sum(dim=−1).mean()
kl_loss = kl_coef × Σ(kl) / batch_size

Why Not Too High?

If entropy_coef is too large, the model generates random outputs to maximize entropy — destroying performance. The default 0.005 is a gentle nudge.

Why Curriculum?

The skip-b task is hard. Starting with length-3 sequences gives the model early reward signal — it can learn to skip 'b' in "abc" before tackling "abcdef". Gradually increasing length is like teaching arithmetic before algebra.

Sequence Sampling
70% of training sequences contain at least one 'b' — ensuring the model sees plenty of cases where it must skip characters.
